      <div><h2>BACKSTORY</h2><ul><li>William Jackson Smart: Civil War vet. His
      wife died in childbirth &amp; he raised 6 children alone.</li><li>His
      daughter Sonora felt inspired to honor him after listening to a Mother’s
      Day sermon (1909) and rallied local support.</li><li>First Father’s Day:
      Spokane, WA, June 19, 1910. The date was inspired by William’s birthday on
      June 5th.</li></ul></div>
    _key: card-2-0924e9ac-d875-421d-b37d-5ba512464325

      <div><h2>Interesting To Note</h2><ul><li>While Mother’s Day became
      official in 1914, Father’s Day did not become *official* until
      1972.</li><li>That said, Father’s Day was observed in various ways around
      the country in the decades in between.</li><li>Pres. Johnson proclaimed
      the 3rd Sunday in June Father’s Day.</li><li>Pres. Nixon signed
      legislation making Father’s Day a national holiday in
      1972.</li></ul></div>
