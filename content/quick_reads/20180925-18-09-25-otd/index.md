      <div><h1>About the Woman:</h1><ul><li>Despite her exceptional academic
      credentials (#2 in her class at Stanford Law), she couldn’t find a law
      firm that would hire a woman. <strong>Her first law job was
      unpaid.</strong></li><li>From 1960-65, she was a <strong>stay-at-home
      mom</strong> to her three sons.</li><li>In 1965, she became the Arizona
      Assistant Attorney General, and later she became the <strong>first female
      Senate Majority Leader</strong> (R) in AZ.</li></ul></div>
    _key: card-3-88bb5417-1d91-46f2-abac-56bb07dd4497

      <div><h1>Her Lasting Legacy:</h1><p>In 1992, she cast the deciding vote in
      <em>Planned Parenthood v. Casey</em>, A upholding the court’s 1973
      decision in <em>Roe v. Wade</em> and striking down a PA law requiring
      women to obtain <strong>spousal consent for abortion</strong>.</p><p>In
      2000, she cast the deciding vote inA <em>Bush v. Gore,</em> settling the
      dispute in the <strong>2000 election</strong> and awarding the presidency
      to George W. Bush.</p></div>
