      <div><h2>Who Names The Storms?</h2><ul><li>Naming handled by <strong>World
      Meteorological Organization</strong>.</li><li>List of names set every 6
      years.</li><li>Assigned alphabetically, alternating men vs. women. Every
      season starts on “A” &amp; rarely goes through entire
      alphabet.</li><li><strong>You’ll never hear of another “Hurricane Katrina”
      because names of infamous deadly and destructive storms are
      retired</strong>.</li></ul></div>
    _key: card-2-39346c03-e94d-434c-8491-661a89d1eee8

      <div><h2>History of Naming</h2><p>Why name? Easy to remember &amp;
      communicate.</p><p>Hurricanes used to be named after saints.</p><p>Storm
      names mainly female in the 1900s; a tradition w/o an
      origin.</p><p>Beginning in 1979, both male &amp; female names officially
      used.</p></div>
