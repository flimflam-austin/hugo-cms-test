      <div><p>America’s <strong>690,000 military spousesA (at least 90% of whom
      are women)</strong>A face unique obstacles finding &amp; keeping jobs due
      to:</p><ul><li>rural base assignments</li><li>unpredictable
      schedules</li><li>limited childcare options</li><li>frequent moves (every
      2-3 years on average)</li><li>occupational licensing regimes that vary by
      state</li></ul></div>
    _key: card-2-c198a349-3198-481c-8b9d-127ed2f8445b
  - order: 2
    useCheckmark: false
    body: >-
      <div><p>As result of the employment difficulties they encounter, military
      spouses are LESS likely than their civilian counterparts to participate in
      the labor market.</p><p>The <strong>unemployment rate</strong> among
      military spouses is estimated to be <strong>AT LEAST 4X HIGHER</strong>A
      than the national average, which was 3.9% in April.</p></div>
